Corporate Lobbying & Bills

Corporate lobbying refers to the practice of companies attempting to influence the decisions and actions of lawmakers, by providing financial support or other resources. This often involves companies using their financial power to persuade lawmakers to introduce, amend or pass legislation that aligns with their interests or to defeat legislation that may negatively impact their business. By using our data, we have collected the top 20 companies who have spent the most on corporate lobbying in the recent quarter to investigate whether these actions can impact their stock prices. Alternatively, by also focusing on bills (as well as companies) we can highlight exactly what bills and legislation acts are of high interest in various companies..
